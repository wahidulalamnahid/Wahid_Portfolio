About Hi, I'm Wahid

Never been just one thing — never stopped trying to be

By day, I test systems for bugs. But my mind doesn't clock out. It wanders through words, sketches feelings in paint, captures silence through film, and questions itself in ink-stained diary pages. I collect coins, not for their value — but for the stories they carry. I write not to impress, but to understand. I’m a polymath by instinct, not title. Every identity I hold — QA engineer, writer, artist, filmmaker, thinker — is just a language I use to explore the same questions: What does it mean to observe deeply? To feel honestly? To live with layered meaning? This site is not a resume. It’s a constellation — of thoughts, colors, mistakes, moments, and maps I’m still drawing. I hope you find something here that mirrors a part of you.

How I Got Here

I didn’t grow up planning to be a polymath. I just followed what felt meaningful. Early on, I was drawn to systems — how they break, how they behave, and what they reveal. That led me into software testing, where I found satisfaction in spotting flaws, tracking logic, and making things better.

But that wasn’t the whole story. I also started painting as a way to express what couldn’t be said out loud. I wrote to understand my thoughts, and eventually directed films to capture emotion visually. Over time, I built a private journal — something I call 23 er Diary — where I reflect on identity, psychology, memory, and emotion. None of these parts are separate. Together, they shape who I am.

The Polymath in Me

QA ENGINEER

I analyze systems to understand how they behave under pressure. I find bugs, track logic, and question structure — not just to fix things, but to understand the deeper patterns of how things work, break, and improve.

WRITER

I write when I need clarity. My words are often personal, reflective, and emotionally honest. Whether it’s a diary entry, a blog post, or a quiet observation, writing helps me process life as it happens.

ARTIST

My paintings are often intuitive. I work with feeling more than form — letting color, space, and contrast speak where language falls short. Art gives me the freedom to be unclear, expressive, and vulnerable.

FILMMAKER

Filmmaking allows me to shape emotion through movement and silence. I use visuals to hold things that are hard to say out loud — memory, mood, stillness. For me, film is a language made of time and attention.

PSYCHOLOGY ENTHUSIAST

Through personal journaling, especially in my 23 er Diary, I explore selfhood, emotional healing, and the invisible parts of being human. I’m interested in how we think, react, hide, and grow — and how we can become more conscious in the process.

COLLECTOR

I collect currencies, books, and objects that feel timeless. Each item carries a story — about a place, a moment, or an era. Collecting is my way of preserving meaning, both personal and cultural.

SEEKER

I question things — not to find the perfect answer, but to understand more deeply. Philosophy, curiosity, and inner reflection guide how I live, learn, and create. I believe questions often carry more truth than conclusions.

Vision

In the next 20 years, I want to create work that lives beyond me. I want to write honest books, make films that move people, create art that speaks without explanation, and build systems that serve humans better. I want to keep exploring — emotionally, intellectually, and creatively — without rushing to arrive. My vision is to stay true to my layered self while building a life that’s both intentional and expressive.

A Few Words I Live By

"You don’t need to choose one path — you need to walk yours fully."

"Some things aren’t meant to be explained. They’re meant to be felt."

"The goal isn’t to be known for everything. It’s to be true in everything you do."
